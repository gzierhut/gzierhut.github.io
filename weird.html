I am not exactly the average person, to say the least. I am not neurotypical, nor do I conform to most of society. Some people may say this is a bad thing. But what is so great about being average? And why should I be ashamed of being different?

I am autistic. This means that I have intense interests, have difficulty understanding social cues and social norms, stim a lot, and am sensitive to noise and light. If I do figure out social norms? I will just reject them.

I am a very quiet person. I only speak when I think it's important to speak or if one of my special interests is brought up. Some people think I should be more talkative, but it's just the way I am.

I do not have conventional interests either. If you looked at my other pages, you know that I'm interested in physics, especially the parts that blow people's minds. I also like philosophy. I like speculating about those questions that are impossible to definitively answer. One side effect is that I have a tendency to get lost in my head. But hey, at least I can easily entertain myself when I'm alone.

And did I tell you I like bad puns? Along with being a complete geek?

I reject the mainstream. First off, I hate the music that is popular right now. I instead like classic rock, especially rock from the 80s. I especially like goth rock, which, contrary to popular belief, is not related to metal, nor associated with Satanists. It is a type of post-punk music with a dark sound and themes.

Ever heard of this guy? Didn't think so.

Second of all, I do not want the same kind of life that people call "a good life". No one should try to fit their life in a box because what people want out of life varies. While I do want a college education, I want to have it not for the money or prestige but rather so that I can work at a job that I love. And I just like educating myself.
